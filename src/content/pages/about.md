AI Attacks documents working AI red team techniques — attack patterns, tooling walkthroughs, and scoping methodology from practitioners who run actual engagements, not simulations.

## What we cover

- **AI red team methodology** — engagement scoping, threat modeling, and the operational discipline that makes assessments reproducible
- **LLM attack patterns** — prompt injection, jailbreaking, context manipulation, and multi-turn attack chains
- **Tooling walkthroughs** — Garak, PyRIT, promptmap, and the rest of the AI red team toolkit
- **Agent and multi-model exploitation** — tool-use abuse, orchestration weaknesses, and agentic attack chains
- **Responsible disclosure** — coordinating findings with vendors and navigating AI vulnerability disclosure

## Who reads this

Red teamers, penetration testers, and security engineers responsible for assessing AI systems. Techniques are reproducible, tools are linked, and results are honest about where attacks fail as well as where they succeed.

## Stay current

Subscribe to the RSS feed for new technique writeups. Contributor submissions are welcome — if you have a reproducible attack worth documenting, contact the editorial desk with appropriate disclosure details.
